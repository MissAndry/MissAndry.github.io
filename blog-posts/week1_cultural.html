Kitchenette

DBC seems like it's actively creating an environment that encourages empathy, curiosity, and rigor. Cohorts are expected to be supportive of each other and of their team, which is something I think I'd be great at as long as I feel that energy from of my fellow cohorts. My expectations haven't changed much - I thought it was going to be very rigorous, and that idea was solidified. The breadth of the engineering empathy program seems larger than I thought, though! It also seems like learning code languages aren't as important as learning a mindset and design concepts, and I'm not sure, anymore, if equal weight is given to front- and back-end development.

Nonetheless, I am excited to learn with passionate, motivated people, even though the thought of constant communication and vulnerability makes me feel... well, nervous. This is a really big change, and I've got the impostor syndrome niggling around my brain.

Either way, I'm mostly excited to make a mess in the kitchen with you all. Let's get cooking.
